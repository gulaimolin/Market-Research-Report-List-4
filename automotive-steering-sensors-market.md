<p><p>Automotive steering sensors are categorized into two main types: contacting sensors and magnetic sensors. Contacting sensors rely on physical contact between components to measure steering angle, while magnetic sensors use magnetic fields to detect motion and determine the steering angle. Contacting sensors are more traditional and cost-effective, but may have higher wear and tear. Magnetic sensors are known for their accuracy and durability, making them more commonly used in modern vehicles. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring accurate and responsive steering control in automobiles.</p></p>
