<p><p>Primary packaging in healthcare refers to the packaging directly in contact with the product, such as bottles or blister packs. It is essential for protecting the product and ensuring proper dosage delivery. Secondary packaging, on the other hand, includes the outer packaging like boxes or cartons that provide additional protection during transportation and storage. Both primary and secondary packaging play crucial roles in maintaining the integrity and safety of healthcare products, ensuring they reach consumers in optimal condition.</p></p>
