<p><p>Injectable bone grafts are used in various orthopedic and dental procedures to promote bone regeneration. There are two main types of injectable bone grafts: natural and synthetic. Natural bone grafts are derived from sources such as autografts or allografts, while synthetic bone grafts are made from materials like calcium phosphate or hydroxyapatite. Natural grafts have the advantage of being biocompatible, while synthetic grafts are often more readily available and cost-effective. Both types have their own strengths and are used based on the specific needs of the patient and procedure.</p></p>
