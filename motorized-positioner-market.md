<p><p>Motorized positioners are devices used to precisely control the position of objects in various applications. Two common types are electric and piezoelectric positioners. Electric positioners are powered by an electric motor, providing smooth and precise movement for applications requiring high accuracy. On the other hand, piezoelectric positioners use piezoelectric material to generate precise movements by applying voltage changes, making them suitable for applications where speed and precision are critical. Both types of positioners are widely used in industries such as semiconductor, optics, and manufacturing.</p></p>
