<p><ul><li>Outboard Joints</li><li>Inboard Joints</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Half Shaft Constant Velocity Joints are classified into two main types based on their position in the vehicle: Outboard Joints and Inboard Joints. Outboard Joints are located at the outer ends of the half shafts and connect them to the wheel hubs, allowing for flexibility and smooth rotation during steering. Inboard Joints are situated closer to the transmission and differential, enabling the half shafts to transmit torque effectively while accommodating vertical movement of the suspension. Both types play crucial roles in maintaining the stability and performance of the vehicle's drivetrain system.</p></p>
