<p><p>Medicinal capsules come in two main types: hard capsules and soft capsules. Hard capsules are made from gelatin or cellulose and are typically used for dry or powdered medications. They are easy to swallow and provide a convenient way to administer medication. Soft capsules, on the other hand, are made from a soft outer shell filled with liquid medication. They are often used for oils, suspensions, or other liquid medications that may be difficult to administer in other forms. Both hard and soft capsules are popular choices in the pharmaceutical industry for delivering a wide range of medicinal compounds.</p></p>
