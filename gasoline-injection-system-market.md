<p><p>Gasoline injection systems are categorized into three main types: mechanical control, electronically controlled, and electromechanical hybrid. Mechanical control systems use mechanical components to control the flow of gasoline into the engine. Electronically controlled systems utilize electronic sensors and actuators to precisely regulate fuel injection. Electromechanical hybrid systems combine both mechanical and electronic components for improved efficiency and performance. Each type of system has its own advantages and challenges, catering to different requirements of vehicles and engine configurations.</p></p>
