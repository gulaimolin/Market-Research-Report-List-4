<p><p>Silicon Carbide Continuous Fibers come in various market types including Pre-ceramic Polymer Pyrolysis (3P), which involves the conversion of polymers into ceramic fibers through heat treatment. Chemical Vapor Deposited (CVD) fibers are produced by chemical reactions and deposits on a substrate. Chemical Vapor Reaction (CVR) fibers are made through chemical reactions between gases. Other types of fibers may include those produced using different methods or combinations of the aforementioned processes. Each market type offers unique characteristics and applications for Silicon Carbide Continuous Fibers.</p></p>
