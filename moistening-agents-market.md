<p><p>Moistening agents are substances added to products to prevent them from drying out. There are two main types of moistening agents: natural and synthetic. Natural moistening agents are derived from plant or animal sources, such as glycerin or honey. Synthetic moistening agents are man-made chemicals designed to mimic the properties of natural substances, such as silicones or propylene glycol. Both types of moistening agents are commonly used in a variety of products, including skincare products, hair care products, and food items.</p></p>
