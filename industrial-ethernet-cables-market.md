<p><p>Industrial Ethernet cables are classified into two main types: copper cables and fiber optic cables. Copper cables are commonly used for shorter distances due to their cost-effectiveness and reliability in industrial environments. They provide high-speed data transmission and are resistant to electromagnetic interference. On the other hand, fiber optic cables are preferred for longer distances as they offer higher bandwidth, longer reach, and better resistance to interference. They are lightweight, immune to electrical interference, and suitable for harsh industrial environments.</p></p>
