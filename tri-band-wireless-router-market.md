<p><p>Tri-band wireless routers operate on three different frequencies to provide faster speeds and reduce interference. 802.11a operates on a 5GHz frequency, 802.11b/g operate on 2.4GHz frequency, and 802.11n/ac operate on both frequencies, offering higher speeds and better range. 802.11n is capable of speeds up to 600Mbps, while 802.11ac can reach speeds up to 1Gbps. These different types cater to varying needs of users, from basic internet browsing to high-bandwidth activities like streaming and gaming.</p></p>
