<p><p>Alfalfa forage is commonly sold in two main market types: dried pulp and wet pulp. Dried pulp is made by drying alfalfa to remove moisture, resulting in a lightweight, easy-to-handle product that is often used as livestock feed. Wet pulp, on the other hand, is made by ensiling the alfalfa with water, creating a more nutrient-dense product that is often used for dairy cattle. Both market types offer unique benefits and are popular choices for feeding a variety of animals.</p></p>
