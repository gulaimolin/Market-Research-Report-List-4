<p><p>Live vaccines are categorized into two main types: monovalent and multivalent. Monovalent vaccines are designed to provide protection against a single specific disease-causing organism, while multivalent vaccines offer protection against multiple strains or types of microorganisms. The monovalent live vaccines market focuses on developing and distributing vaccines for individual diseases, while the multivalent live vaccines market targets a broader range of diseases with a single vaccine. Both types play a crucial role in preventing infectious diseases and promoting public health.</p></p>
