<p><p>Injectable targeted therapy refers to medications that selectively target specific molecules involved in disease progression. There are different types of injectable targeted therapy, including monoclonal antibodies, small molecules, immunotoxins, and others. Monoclonal antibodies are proteins that target specific cell surface receptors. Small molecules are low molecular weight compounds that inhibit certain cellular processes. Immunotoxins combine antibodies with toxins to selectively kill cancer cells. Other targeted therapies may include gene therapy, RNA-based therapies, and nanoparticle-based therapies.</p></p>
