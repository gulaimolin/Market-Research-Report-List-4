<p><p>Laboratory stereoscopic microscopes come in three main types: monocular, binocular, and trinocular. Monocular stereoscopic microscopes have only one eyepiece, making them more affordable and suitable for basic tasks. Binocular stereoscopic microscopes have two eyepieces, providing a more comfortable viewing experience and depth perception. Trinocular stereoscopic microscopes have three eyepieces, allowing for simultaneous viewing and image capture. Each type of microscope is suitable for different applications depending on the level of detail and functionality required in laboratory settings.</p></p>
