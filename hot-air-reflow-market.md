<p><p>Hot Air Reflow is a technique used in the soldering of surface-mount electronic components on printed circuit boards. This process involves using a stream of hot air to heat up the solder paste, melting it and creating a strong bond between the component and PCB. Hot Air Reflow is widely used in electronics manufacturing due to its efficiency, precision, and environmental friendliness compared to traditional soldering methods.</p><p>The Hot Air Reflow Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.5% during the forecast period.
