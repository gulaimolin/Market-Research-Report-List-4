<p><p>Conjugate vaccines are categorized into two types: monovalent and multivalent. Monovalent conjugate vaccines target a single specific strain or serotype of a bacteria or virus. Multivalent conjugate vaccines, on the other hand, target multiple strains or serotypes of a pathogen in a single vaccine formulation. These vaccines are developed to provide protection against a broader range of disease-causing strains, making them more effective in preventing infections and reducing the spread of diseases.</p></p>
